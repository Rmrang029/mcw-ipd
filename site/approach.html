Our IPD Approach

How MCW shows up under an integrated contract.

A practical view of what we bring to the Big Room, the design phase, and construction — written for owners and architects evaluating MCW for an IPD pursuit.

Starting position

IPD is a behaviour, not a contract clause.

The contract sets the rules for risk, reward, and accountability. The behaviour is what determines whether the project actually delivers value. MCW operates under CCDC 30 and equivalent IPD agreements with that distinction in mind: we treat the agreement as the floor, not the ceiling.

What that looks like in practice: senior engineers in the Big Room with authority to commit, real-time model updates rather than a build-up to a milestone, and an explicit commitment to share information that affects target cost as soon as it becomes known.

Operating principles

  • Co-design with trades, not for them.
  • Decisions made at the right level — not escalated.
  • Disclose pressure on target cost early.
  • Treat lessons learned as design input, not retrospective reading.
The mechanics

Five practices we treat as non-negotiable.

Big Room cadence

Regular co-located sessions for transparent decision-making. We staff with seniors who can commit on the spot — never with junior placeholders waiting for direction.

PIT teams

Process Improvement Teams (or equivalent task-specific groups) tackle scope, schedule, and budget questions in parallel. We participate where mechanical or electrical decisions sit on the critical path.

Target Value Design

Design to a target cost, not toward a budget. We bring lifecycle thinking — energy modelling, system right-sizing, and constructability — to keep the team out of value-engineering rework cycles.

BIM-first coordination

Models are the source of truth, kept current in near-real time. Clashes are resolved in the model, with the trade contractor in the room — not negotiated in the field.

Lean methods

Last Planner, pull planning, and structured A3 problem-solving. Used as tools, not theatre. The aim is fewer surprises, faster commitments, and a flatter handoff into construction.

ICL discipline

The Incentive Compensation Layer aligns incentives only if everyone respects the math. We hold our scope honestly under shared pool arrangements and flag risk to the pool early.

By phase

Where we add the most value.

Phase What MCW does Why it matters under IPD
Validation Confirm program against mechanical and electrical reality. Energy and load calculations. Site servicing constraints. Risk register. Validates that the target cost is buildable. Surfaces hidden constraints before TVD locks in.
Schematic Design System concept selection, energy model, constructability review with trade partners, early specification framing. System choices made here drive 70% of lifecycle cost. We make them with the contractor in the room.
Design Development BIM coordination, equipment selection, controls strategy, life-safety integration, prefab opportunities. BIM coordination at this stage is what stops field rework. We invest the hours here so construction does not pay for them later.
Implementation Documents Drawings, specs, schedules of equipment, commissioning framework, contractor handover packages. Documents that the trades and contractor have already endorsed. No "permit set vs. construction set" surprise.
Construction RFI cadence, model maintenance, witness testing, commissioning support, real-time as-builts. Continuity of relationships from design. The same engineer who drew it answers in the field.
Closeout & Handover Commissioning verification, owner training, lessons-learned capture, documented warranty positioning. Closes the loop. Lessons feed directly into the next pursuit so we do not lose the team's accumulated learning.
A note on tools

BIM, lean, and energy modelling are tools — not the point.

The point is a building that performs and a team that finishes the project on better terms than it started. We deploy BIM, energy modelling, lean planning, and target value design where they earn their keep, and we resist the impulse to add process for its own sake.

Every IPD project we have delivered has been better off because the team agreed early on what they were trying to optimise. The tools followed.

Standards we work under

  • CCDC 30 — Canadian IPD contract framework.
  • BC Building Code, ASHRAE 90.1 / 62.1, NFPA standards.
  • LEED BD+C, Passive House, WELL where target.
  • Engineers and Geoscientists BC professional practice guidelines.
