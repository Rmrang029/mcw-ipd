Overview

The GO Transit Streetsville Bus Facility is a design-build transit operations facility completed in 2009. At approximately 20,264 m² and certified LEED Silver, the facility supports GO bus operations and required building systems suited to vehicle-related operations, maintenance support, and a public transit operating environment.

MCW provided mechanical and electrical engineering services for the Streetsville facility under the design-build team alongside Strasman Architects and RJC Engineers (structural).

MCW's Role

MCW's scope addressed the operational complexity of a bus maintenance and operations facility: vehicle-related ventilation (exhaust capture and make-up air), maintenance bay infrastructure, fueling and wash systems integration, life-safety, lighting and lighting controls, electrical distribution to bay and shop equipment, and the administrative and operations support spaces. Systems were sized for continuous, heavy-duty operational use.

How Design-Build Was Applied

Transit operations design-build requires the engineering team to commit early to systems sized and specified for a working maintenance and storage environment. MCW's scope was integrated into the contractor-led team from concept through commissioning, with engineering decisions held against the consortium's combined cost, schedule, and operational performance commitments.

Why this project matters

This project is a useful long-term transit facility reference, especially for proposals involving bus operations, maintenance functions, and design-build coordination.
