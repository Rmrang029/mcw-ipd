Kamloops Centre for the Arts

Location · Kamloops, BC
Delivery · Integrated Project Delivery
MCW Role · Electrical Engineering Consultant

Overview

A $211M landmark civic and cultural facility for the City of Kamloops, delivered under a true Integrated Project Delivery framework with MCW serving as the lead electrical engineering consultant on a 14-partner integrated team.

Located at 393 Seymour Street next to Kelson Hall — home to Western Canada Theatre and Kamloops Symphony — the Kamloops Centre for the Arts is the first project under the City's Build Kamloops capital initiative. The facility is designed to anchor a new arts and cultural precinct in the downtown core, with construction broken ground in 2025 and target completion in 2029.

The program brings together two state-of-the-art theatres, a public-facing café and gallery, an expansive lobby and back-of-house, and two levels of underground parking accommodating 158 vehicles. The building is designed to be inclusive, accessible, and operationally efficient over its full lifecycle.

MCW's Role

MCW is the lead electrical engineering consultant under the IPD agreement. Our scope covers the full electrical design across power distribution, lighting and lighting controls, life-safety systems, communications and low-voltage infrastructure, and theatre-specific electrical requirements coordinated with the theatre design consultant.

As an integrated team member, MCW participates in Big Room sessions, PIT (Project Implementation Team) groups, and joint constructability reviews with the contractor and trade partners. Our electrical scope is held under the team's shared target cost and risk-reward pool.

How IPD Was Applied

Fourteen-partner integrated team. The KCA IPD agreement brings together the Owner, architect, general contractor, key engineering disciplines, theatre and acoustic specialists, and key trades under one shared-risk, shared-reward delivery structure.

The team includes the City of Kamloops (Owner), Station One Architects (Architect), Clark Builders (General Contractor), Collins Steel, Westcana Electric, Kerr Interior, Interior Plumbing & Heating, WSP + Rocky Point Engineering, Aercoustics, MCW Consultants, WSP + Fast + Epp, DWD Theatre Design, True Consulting, Weft Strategies, and VDM Consulting.

This breadth of disciplines — in particular the inclusion of theatre and acoustic specialists alongside structural, civil, and trade partners — means that critical decisions about electrical infrastructure (rigging power, dimming and control, audio/visual integration, broadcast cabling) are made with the people who will install and operate them in the room.

MCW's electrical scope under the IPD framework includes early validation of utility capacity and service strategy, energy modelling input to support sustainability targets, lighting and controls design coordinated against architectural and theatre design intent, and constructability coordination with Westcana Electric (electrical trade partner) through the model.

Why this project matters

The Kamloops Centre for the Arts is one of the most significant cultural infrastructure projects underway in interior British Columbia. Its delivery under a true IPD framework — with a fully integrated 14-party team — positions it as a reference project for how complex civic facilities can be delivered with cost certainty and design quality alongside a transparent collaborative process.

For MCW, the project demonstrates our ability to lead electrical engineering on a major IPD pursuit alongside national-scale partners, with disciplined scope, schedule, and budget accountability under the shared risk-reward agreement.
