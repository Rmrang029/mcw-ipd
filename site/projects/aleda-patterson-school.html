Overview

Aleda Patterson School (K-3) was delivered alongside Alex Janvier School (grades 4-9) under a single IPD project for Edmonton Public Schools, with each school featuring a 210 kW solar PV array offsetting more than 50% of energy use.

The project included demolition of the existing Westlawn and Afton School buildings and complete site servicing infrastructure for both schools, alongside design, costing, energy assessments, modelling, and installation of solar technology.

Both schools share the same IPD framework, incentive structure, and design culture — with parallel program objectives tailored to the age groups served and identical sustainability ambitions.

MCW's Role

MCW served as Electrical Engineer under the IPD agreement, with scope including the photovoltaic system design, integration into the building electrical distribution, and supporting energy modelling and assessments.

How IPD Was Applied

Single team, two schools. The IPD agreement covered both schools as a coordinated delivery, allowing shared site servicing infrastructure, coordinated design culture, and integrated incentive structures across the pair.

Key participants worked collaboratively to document project objectives at the outset and establish targets serving as metrics for compensation adjustments and goals for Target Value Design. Such objectives and targets included program criteria, standards, target costs, schedule, ICL participation, and key milestones for periodic ICL distribution.

Edmonton Public Schools retained ultimate control over project scope and quality, with all work conducted to EPS standards. The IPD framework supported integrated decision-making on system electrification, on-site solar generation, and energy efficiency strategies.

Project Objectives

  • A building that maximises energy efficiency and sustainability to the full extent of the budget.
  • A building that achieves LEED Silver or better with potential to incorporate alternative energy systems.
  • A building recognised as innovative in school design, supporting 21st century learning environments.
  • A building that fosters a strong sense of community and school pride.
  • Site and building design elements that conform to the latest Edmonton Public Schools Design Guidelines.
