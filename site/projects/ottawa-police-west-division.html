Overview

The Ottawa Police Service West Division Patrol Station is a 42,000-sf policing facility delivered for the Ottawa Police Service under a construction management arrangement. The project required phased submissions and phased tender/construction packages, creating a delivery environment where engineering design had to remain coordinated with active procurement and construction sequencing.

MCW provided mechanical and electrical engineering services for the patrol station, working alongside the construction manager through phased package delivery.

MCW's Role

MCW's mechanical and electrical scope addressed the operational and security requirements of a working police patrol station: secure operational areas, controlled-access infrastructure, communications and IT systems, resilient power and life-safety, and systems sized for 24/7 operation. Phased package delivery required disciplined coordination so that engineering scope, specifications, and drawings stayed consistent across multiple tender packages.

How Construction Management Was Applied

Construction Management as a delivery model places the construction manager as the owner's agent for procurement and construction, with engineering scope often phased to support package-based tender. MCW's role was to keep the engineering scope coordinated and consistent across phases, and to remain responsive to active procurement and construction decisions in real time.

Why this project matters

This profile is valuable for proposals where the owner expects consultant flexibility, early package coordination, and engineering discipline under a construction-management or CM-at-risk-style environment.
