Township of Langley Fire Hall & Training Centre

Location · Langley, BC
Delivery · Integrated Project Delivery
MCW Role · Mechanical & Electrical Engineer

Overview

A state-of-the-art Fire Hall and Training Centre delivered through an Integrated Project Delivery approach, designed to enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of the Township's fire services while supporting advanced training and rapid response capabilities.

The Township of Langley is developing a fully equipped fire hall and a dedicated training facility with simulation environments, classrooms, and state-of-the-art training tools. The project supports both daily operations and ongoing firefighter development under a single integrated facility.

Sustainability is a project priority. The team is incorporating eco-friendly building practices, energy-efficient systems, and durable materials to minimise environmental impact across the facility lifecycle.

Facility Type

Sustainability
Energy-efficient systems, sustainable materials

Operational + simulation training

MCW's Role

MCW provides the full mechanical and electrical engineering scope, integrated into the IPD team from validation through to construction. Our seniors participate in Big Room sessions and PIT teams covering scope, schedule, and budget.

How IPD Was Applied

Collaboration as the operating principle. The IPD method has been used to align stakeholders — owner, contractor, architect, and engineering disciplines — around shared targets and shared accountability for delivery.

By involving every stakeholder early and throughout, the team makes informed decisions that balance operational performance, training capability, and lifecycle cost. Modern firefighting technology and equipment, training simulators, classrooms, and apparatus bays are coordinated against mechanical and electrical service strategies in the model rather than in the field.

Our role is to keep the mechanical and electrical scope honest under the target cost — surfacing trade-offs early so the team can make informed choices, rather than absorbing surprises later in construction.

Goals the IPD Team is Pursuing

  • Improve emergency response times and overall service quality.
  • Provide a comprehensive training centre for ongoing firefighter readiness.
  • Increase community safety through improved fire-services infrastructure.
  • Deliver on time and within target cost via the IPD approach.
