Overview

The Stoney Compressed Natural Gas Bus Storage and Transit Facility is a major Calgary transit facility delivered under a DBFM public-private partnership. The facility became operational in February 2019 and supports the City's transition to compressed natural gas bus operations.

MCW provided mechanical and electrical engineering for the CNG transit facility under the consortium delivery agreement, addressing the specific code, ventilation, and gas-detection requirements associated with CNG bus storage alongside fleet maintenance, wash, fuelling, and back-of-house systems.

MCW's Role

MCW's scope coordinated CNG-specific mechanical requirements (ventilation, gas detection, dispensing infrastructure, code compliance) with general fleet operations (vehicle wash, maintenance bays, parts storage, administrative areas). Electrical scope addressed power, lighting, classified-area requirements, and life-safety systems suitable to the CNG environment.

How DBFM Was Applied

A long-term DBFM agreement put the consortium responsible for design, construction, financing, and ongoing maintenance — meaning building systems were designed not just for capital cost but for full-lifecycle operational and maintenance burden. Operability and maintainability were tested directly against the consortium's own long-term obligation.

Why this project matters

This profile is relevant to transit, fleet, and alternative-fuel infrastructure pursuits where operational uptime, maintainability, and integrated building systems are key evaluation themes.
