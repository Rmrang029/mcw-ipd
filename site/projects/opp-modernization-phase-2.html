Overview

OPP Modernization Phase 2 continued Ontario's investment in modern OPP facilities through a DBF public-private partnership. The program included new detachments and specialized OPP facilities across multiple Ontario communities, building on the portfolio-delivery lessons from Phase 1.

MCW delivered mechanical and electrical engineering across the Phase 2 portfolio under the Bird Capital LP design-build-finance team. Multi-site delivery required repeatable engineering standards, consistent quality, and disciplined coordination with the constructor across geographically distributed sites.

MCW's Role

MCW's mechanical and electrical scope covered detachments and specialized OPP facilities across the portfolio. Standardization of engineering approach was central: repeatable system designs, consistent specifications, and coordinated documentation across sites supported predictable delivery for the consortium and the public owner.

How DBF Was Applied

The DBF model put the consortium on the hook for design, construction, and financing through delivery (operations remain with the public owner). Engineering decisions were made within a fixed-price, fixed-schedule envelope, with disciplined coordination required across multiple parallel sites.

Why this project matters

This profile reinforces MCW's experience supporting repeat P3 public-safety programs, including work across multi-site portfolios with standardized operational and technical requirements.
