Overview

Phase 2 of Sheridan College's Hazel McCallion Campus expanded the Mississauga campus through a fixed-price design-build-finance P3/AFP contract. The project reached substantial completion in late 2016 and opened to students in 2017, adding major academic capacity within an urban campus setting.

MCW served as the mechanical, electrical, IT, audiovisual, and energy modelling consultant under the Infrastructure Ontario AFP framework, working alongside the design-build consortium to deliver coordinated systems within fixed-price, fixed-schedule constraints typical of P3 academic delivery.

MCW's Role

MCW provided full mechanical, electrical, IT, audiovisual, and energy modelling consulting services on the Phase 2 expansion. Engineering decisions were tested against budget and schedule certainty alongside long-term operational performance under the AFP framework.

How AFP Was Applied

Under Infrastructure Ontario's AFP / DBF model, the consortium assumes design and construction risk against a fixed price and schedule. MCW's scope was integrated into that consortium delivery, with engineering decisions exposed to early coordination with the constructor and trade partners rather than handed off after design completion.

Why this project matters

This project is a strong reference for clients seeking MCW experience on P3/AFP post-secondary buildings where budget certainty, schedule certainty, and operational performance are central procurement drivers.
