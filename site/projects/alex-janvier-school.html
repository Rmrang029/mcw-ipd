Alex Janvier School

Location · Edmonton, AB
Delivery · Integrated Project Delivery
MCW Role · Electrical Engineer

Overview

An Edmonton Public Schools IPD project covering the design and construction of two schools in the Westlawn community: Alex Janvier (grades 4-9) and Aleda Patterson (K-3). The project replaced the existing Westlawn and Afton School buildings with new facilities incorporating significant solar PV capacity.

The project included demolition of the existing Westlawn and Afton School buildings and complete site servicing infrastructure for both schools, alongside design, costing, energy assessments, modelling, and installation of solar technology.

A standout feature is that each school's solar array comprises 210 kW of solar PV capacity — subsidising more than 50% of each school's energy needs.

MCW's Role

MCW served as Electrical Engineer under the IPD agreement, with scope including the photovoltaic system design, integration into the building electrical distribution, and supporting energy modelling and assessments.

How IPD Was Applied

Targets define behaviour. Key participants worked collaboratively to document project objectives at the outset and establish targets that serve as metrics for compensation adjustments and goals for Target Value Design.

Such objectives and targets included program criteria, standards, target costs, schedule, Incentive Compensation Layer (ICL) participation, and key milestones for periodic ICL distribution. Edmonton Public Schools retained ultimate control over project scope and quality, with all work conducted to EPS standards.

The IPD framework allowed the project team to make integrated decisions on system electrification, on-site solar generation, and energy efficiency strategies — rather than treating sustainability as a layer to be added once the design was set.

Project Objectives

  • A building that maximises energy efficiency and sustainability to the full extent of the budget.
  • A building that achieves LEED Silver or better with potential to incorporate alternative energy systems.
  • A building recognised as innovative in school design, supporting 21st century learning environments.
  • A building that fosters a strong sense of community and school pride.
  • Site and building design elements that conform to the latest Edmonton Public Schools Design Guidelines.
