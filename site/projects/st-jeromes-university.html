St. Jerome's University Campus Development

Location · Waterloo, ON
Delivery · Integrated Project Delivery
MCW Role · Mechanical & Electrical Engineer

Overview

St. Jerome's University (SJU) is undertaking an extensive campus renewal project to enhance its academic and residential facilities — delivered through a comprehensive Integrated Project Delivery framework. The development includes new academic centres and residence towers designed around community and learning.

The Academic Centre is a modern facility with classrooms in 300, 125, 75, and 60-seat configurations, plus multipurpose spaces for student lounges and study. The residence towers are two- to three-storey brick buildings organised in clusters to create a community-focused living environment, accommodating 360 beds in a mix of single and double rooms.

Ground-floor amenity spaces include lounges, study areas, music and creative rooms, kitchens, laundry, fitness, and gymnasium — supporting diverse learning and social interaction across the campus.

MCW's Role

MCW provided the full mechanical and electrical engineering scope under the IPD agreement, with active participation in Big Room meetings, PIT teams, and BIM-led coordination across all disciplines.

How IPD Was Applied

Big Room cadence with PIT teams. The IPD approach promotes collaboration among all stakeholders, ensuring efficient project execution and value for money.

Key elements include regular Big Room meetings for transparent communication and decision-making, and PIT teams — task-specific groups responsible for budget, scope of work, and schedule management. MCW participates where mechanical or electrical decisions sit on the critical path.

Extensive use of Building Information Modelling (BIM) enhances design coordination and project management. The project's emphasis on eco-friendly practices, durable materials, and energy-efficient systems is supported by integrated multidisciplinary engineering decisions made within the model rather than in committee.

Key Components
