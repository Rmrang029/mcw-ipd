Overview

The Alouette Correctional Centre for Women is a secure justice-sector facility delivered through design-build for the BC Ministry of Justice. The project required M&E systems that could support safety, supervision, resilience, controlled access, and day-to-day operations in a correctional environment.

MCW provided mechanical and electrical engineering for the Alouette Correctional Centre under the design-build team. Scope addressed correctional-specific requirements including secure systems, controlled-access design, supervision-supporting infrastructure, and resilient day-to-day operations.

MCW's Role

MCW's scope coordinated correctional-specific systems requirements (secure equipment locations, controlled access, supervision-supporting CCTV/comm infrastructure, resilient power and ventilation) with the broader operational program. Engineering decisions were made with attention to durability and uptime under heavy continuous use.

How Design-Build Was Applied

Justice-sector design-build couples standard capital constraints with elevated security, safety, and code requirements. MCW's role was to translate operational and security needs into M/E systems that meet code, support supervision, and remain operable under sustained heavy use.

Why this project matters

This project is well suited to justice, corrections, and public-safety proposals where clients need assurance that M&E design can support secure operations under a design-build framework.
