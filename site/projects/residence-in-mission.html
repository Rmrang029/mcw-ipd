Overview

The Residence in Mission is a 200-bed long-term care facility for Fraser Health Authority, completed in 2014. The campus of care provides eight residential “neighbourhoods” of 25 beds each, organized over three floors, designed to support residents requiring complex care, dementia support, respite, and behaviour transitional support.

MCW provided mechanical and electrical engineering for the facility under the VanMar Constructors design-build team alongside KMBR Architects. Scope addressed clinical-grade systems suited to long-term care, with attention to comfort, safety, accessibility, and reliable long-term operation.

MCW's Role

MCW's mechanical and electrical scope addressed long-term care environment requirements: HVAC sized for resident comfort and clinical needs, lighting and lighting controls calibrated for older adults, life-safety and emergency systems, electrical distribution and emergency power, and dementia-supportive infrastructure such as wander-prevention and security systems. Engineering decisions accommodated the home-like aesthetic of the residence-style facility while still meeting healthcare-grade reliability and code expectations.

How Design-Build Was Applied

Healthcare design-build for long-term care couples standard capital constraints with elevated comfort, safety, and clinical performance requirements. MCW's role was to translate operational and clinical needs into M/E systems that meet code, support the resident-centred care model, and remain operable with manageable facility staffing over the long term.

Why this project matters

This profile is suitable for healthcare and seniors-care proposals where clients need evidence of MCW experience with design-build delivery and operationally sensitive residential-care environments.
