Overview

An asset lifecycle extension and decarbonisation program at Robson Square — a 1.3 million-square-foot, three-block downtown Vancouver landmark housing the Law Courts, UBC's downtown campus, an ice rink, and the Vancouver Art Gallery. MCW leads the multidisciplinary consulting team as Prime Consultant for the Province of BC.

Robson Square is an architectural landmark completed in the 1980s, spanning three city blocks in downtown Vancouver — Blocks 51, 61, and 71. Constructed with reinforced concrete, steel, and glass, the complex provides approximately 1.3 million square feet of functional space.

In 2022 the Ministry of Citizens' Services engaged MCW to lead the Robson Square Asset Life Cycle Extension Project, encompassing mechanical, electrical, and lighting systems renewal. Acting as the central point of coordination for the client and sub-consultants, MCW ensures effective communication, alignment with stakeholder expectations, and a seamless, efficient delivery process. The program builds upon recommendations from the 2019 feasibility study and the subsequent low-carbon electrification study, targeting significant reductions in the facility's carbon footprint through low-carbon and renewable technology integration.

MCW's Role

MCW leads the multidisciplinary consulting team as Prime Consultant on the Robson Square program, with sub-consultants including Hanscomb and O4 Architecture coordinated under MCW.

Mechanical scope includes modernization of HVAC, plumbing, and fire protection systems to enhance occupant comfort, safety, and performance while minimizing energy use. Electrical scope addresses power distribution, emergency power, and communication systems — ensuring regulatory compliance and future-ready performance for the long-lived landmark.

In parallel, MCW developed a lighting and lighting control feasibility study and is implementing advanced energy-efficient lighting solutions that reduce consumption, elevate visual quality, and contribute to a safer, more inviting environment across the public-facing and operational spaces of the complex.

How CMAR Was Applied

Active landmark, multi-tenant complex. Robson Square houses the Law Courts, UBC's downtown campus, an ice rink, and the Vancouver Art Gallery — all of which must remain operational throughout design and construction.

The project is structured under a Construction Management at Risk approach to align engineering scope, lifecycle extension, and decarbonisation with the Province's CleanBC initiative. The construction manager carries cost risk against the target while MCW, as Prime Consultant, leads design coordination across the multi-block, multi-tenant landmark facility — keeping operations continuous and stakeholders aligned through design and procurement.

MCW's design approach prioritizes sustainability, energy efficiency, and long-term resilience, integrating innovative low-carbon and renewable technologies. Working collaboratively with provincial and municipal stakeholders, MCW supports the Province's commitment to sustainable infrastructure renewal — extending the lifespan of this civic landmark while advancing decarbonisation goals.

Why this project matters

Robson Square is one of British Columbia's most recognizable civic landmarks. MCW's role as Prime Consultant on its asset lifecycle extension and decarbonisation program demonstrates the firm's capability to lead multidisciplinary teams on complex, occupied, multi-tenant government landmarks where energy, sustainability, lifecycle, and continuous operations all factor heavily into design decisions.

The project is a strong reference for clients seeking proven Prime Consultant experience on large institutional and government portfolios where decarbonisation goals must be reconciled with operational continuity, public access, and tenant program protection.
