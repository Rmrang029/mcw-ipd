Burnaby RCMP Detachment

Location · Burnaby, BC
Delivery · Integrated Project Delivery
MCW Role · Mechanical Engineer

Overview

A new 120,556-sf RCMP detachment serving the City of Burnaby, delivered through an Integrated Project Delivery framework with shared risk and reward across the full design and construction team.

The current Burnaby RCMP building is 53 years old (with a wing added in 1981) and houses 409 staff — 301 Police and 108 City employees. In 2020, the City conducted an RCMP Detachment Space Need Study to develop a clear and comprehensive understanding of current space needs and a renewal plan for the next 20 years. It established that a new building of approximately 120,556 sf / 11,200 sm is required to accommodate the Detachment's future growth.

In February 2023, the Council approved a location at Norland and Ledger for the new Burnaby RCMP Detachment, with at least two vehicle access points and confirmed parking requirements.

MCW's Role

MCW is part of a highly collaborative IPD design and construction team, sharing the project's risks and rewards. Our scope covers the full mechanical design including HVAC, plumbing, and fire protection systems, with active participation in design charrettes and Big Room sessions.

The IPD team will validate the functional program space needs as the RCMP reviews and refines requirements, while the IPD Representative Consultant supports and assists the City to design and construct the new detachment. MCW will perform energy and load calculations for the various mechanical systems and remain accountable for performance through delivery.

How IPD Was Applied

Shared risk and reward. MCW operates under the IPD agreement with full participation in the team's risk-reward pool. Our scope and incentives align with the project outcome, not with our own deliverable count.

MCW works closely with the IPD team to identify, assess, and mitigate potential risks related to the mechanical consulting scope, implementing a proactive risk management strategy to address potential challenges and ensure the project achieves its objectives.

We establish and track key performance metrics related to the mechanical scope — energy efficiency, system performance, and cost effectiveness — and engage actively in feedback and lessons-learned sessions to identify opportunities for improvement and streamline workflows.

The team has defined the objectives, performance requirements, and budget constraints to establish a shared goal understanding and set the stage for value-driven decision-making throughout design. We engage all relevant stakeholders in a collaborative design process, leveraging diverse perspectives to develop innovative and cost-effective mechanical design solutions that meet the project objectives and the Owner's needs.

Target Value Design Discipline

During design, the team identifies and mitigates potential risks that could impact costs and value, helping to maintain the target cost and ensure the project delivers the desired value to the Owner. Energy modelling, system right-sizing, and lifecycle cost analysis are deployed early to inform system selection rather than to validate decisions already made.
