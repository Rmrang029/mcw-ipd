Overview

Completed in June 2017, St. Joseph High School in Red Deer was the first school project in Canada to be delivered using Integrated Project Delivery. A 12,133 m² facility for 900 students with expansion capability for an additional 300, achieving LEED Silver certification.

The greenfield site was developed not only for the school's program requirements but also to accommodate key partnerships with local clubs and associations. The high school was intended to support community programs from athletic clubs to marching bands, with accommodations reflecting these relationships.

The school program incorporated and expanded real-world learning opportunities such as media studies, robotics, culinary arts, and fabrication. The project achieved LEED Silver Certification with the project team — including significant involvement from Owner representatives — focusing on creating sustainable designs that deliver long-term value for the School Board.

MCW's Role

MCW served as Mechanical Engineer of Record under the IPD agreement. We participated as a fully integrated member of the design and construction team, sharing in the project's risk-reward pool and operating under the team's shared target cost.

How IPD Was Applied

Innovative early-execution decision. Three external community partners — the Kinsman Club, City of Red Deer, and Catholic School Board — agreed to fund a garage built early in the project. The structure first served as the on-site Big Room and project coordination office during construction, then was turned over to the partners at completion to address their storage needs.

The early-build garage decision did not affect the project completion date because of the flexibility of the IPD process. It is a useful illustration of how IPD enables creative funding and program structures that traditional contracts make difficult or impossible.

The team operated with significant Owner representative involvement throughout, with sustainable design decisions made collaboratively against the LEED Silver target and the long-term operational interests of the School Board.

Why this project matters

Beyond the building itself, St. Joseph established a delivery culture and team familiarity that compounded directly into the next IPD project — St. Gregory the Great. The lessons learned framework allowed the same team to extend the IPD approach with measurably better execution on the second project.
