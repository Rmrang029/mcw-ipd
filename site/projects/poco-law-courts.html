Overview

A $22M renovation and lifecycle renewal of the Port Coquitlam Law Courts — a live, operational justice facility — expanding Supreme Court functionality for the Tri-Cities area while delivering CleanBC-aligned mechanical, electrical, and security upgrades. MCW provides M/E and ICAT/IMIT (technology) services as sub-consultant under O4 Architecture (Prime).

The Ministries of Attorney General and Public Safety & Solicitor General identified the need to maximize courthouse utilization and repurpose less-used courtrooms to accommodate the Supreme Court's evolving needs. In parallel, the Ministry of Citizens' Services confirmed an imminent need for lifecycle extension work, with many building systems having reached the end of their useful life. The CleanBC initiative further recognized the potential to leverage this site for energy and greenhouse gas savings.

MCW's Role

MCW provides mechanical, electrical, and ICAT/IMIT (technology) engineering services as part of the O4 Architecture-led prime consultant team.

Mechanical scope addresses HVAC, plumbing, and fire protection renewal coordinated to support renovated courtrooms, judicial chambers, registry, sheriff, and administrative areas, with energy-efficient strategies aligned with CleanBC. Electrical scope covers power distribution, emergency power, lighting and lighting controls, life-safety, and security infrastructure. ICAT/IMIT scope addresses upgraded courtroom IT, audiovisual, and communication systems for a live, mission-critical justice facility.

The renovation will repurpose Courtrooms 10, 11, and 12 to create a Supreme Jury Courtroom, jury deliberation suite, and a secure accused holding dock, alongside upgrades to judicial chambers, scheduling, registry, barristers' lounge, and sheriff/court administration. Two judicial chambers will be repurposed for a Supreme Court meeting/break room and a Judicial Administrative Assistants work area. The project also accommodates the growth of the BC Prosecution Service within the vacated Family Justice Services space on the lower level.

How CMAR Was Applied

Three-phase delivery on an active courthouse. Phase 1 renovation pre-planning; Phase 2 lifecycle upgrade planning and design (exterior, interior, mechanical, electrical); Phase 3 renovation and lifecycle upgrade implementation.

The CMAR delivery approach allows MCW's design scope to be coordinated with the construction manager's cost and schedule commitments, with engineering decisions tested against operational continuity for an active courthouse and against the Province's CleanBC, security, and stewardship standards.

The project is designed in accordance with the Province's technical and environmental standards, including the SSBC Environmental Stewardship Policy, BC Sheriff Service Security System Requirements, and RPD Physical Security Standard 2019. MCW's M/E and ICAT/IMIT design must reconcile lifecycle renewal cost-effectiveness with secure, mission-grade operational requirements throughout phased construction.

Why this project matters

The Port Coquitlam Law Courts renovation demonstrates MCW's ability to deliver complex M/E and ICAT/IMIT scope on an operational justice facility under CMAR, while meeting BC's elevated security standards and CleanBC sustainability objectives.

For proposals involving justice, public-safety, and operational government renovation, this project shows MCW's capacity to coordinate phased design with the construction manager and prime architectural consultant while keeping a live, mission-critical facility operational throughout the work.
