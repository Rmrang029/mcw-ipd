Overview

Located at 100 Ave NW and 182 St NW, the new AUPE Headquarters is a four-storey, 120,000-sf modern commercial building completed in 2020. Delivered under a modified Design-Build approach with extensive BIM use and a highly collaborative process, including LEED registration and one of Canada's earliest WELL Building Standard certifications.

The structure makes use of green materials and mechanical systems — including solar power — to reduce the office block's carbon footprint and heating/cooling costs. Inside, AUPE HQ is airy and bright, with its asymmetrical footprint creating irregular but efficient spaces. Natural light was a major design factor, with ample windows and skylights bringing the outdoors in.

The new AUPE Corporate Headquarters is a unique project built using a modified Design-Build approach, relying on Building Information Modelling and a highly collaborative design and construction process. It demonstrates how IPD-aligned behaviour can be applied effectively even outside a CCDC 30 IPD agreement.

MCW's Role

MCW Hemisphere and Arpi's provided mechanical solutions for this collaboratively delivered project, working alongside the integrated design-build team to deliver mechanical systems supporting both LEED registration and WELL certification.

How IPD Was Applied

Expedited permitting through collaboration. The project team worked closely with the City of Edmonton to establish both Development and Building Permitting well ahead of typical submissions — advancing both design timeframe and construction significantly for a building of this size and complexity.

This involved providing City Inspectors with details of the intended design covering key life-safety systems and commentary on other systems still in design. Information focused on the requirements needed for sign-off, with updates to the City coming as designs were confirmed.

The collaborative model, supported by BIM as the source of truth, illustrates how the cultural elements of IPD — transparency, early integration with regulators, real-time information sharing — deliver value even when the contract form is Design-Build rather than IPD proper.

Why this project belongs in our IPD experience

AUPE is included in this portfolio because the delivery culture — integrated team, BIM-first coordination, expedited approvals through transparent regulator engagement — reflects the same operating principles MCW brings into pure IPD agreements. It is a useful demonstration that the value of IPD is in the behaviour, not only in the contract.
