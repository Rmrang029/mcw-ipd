Overview

The Saskatoon Police Headquarters is a major public-safety facility delivered on a design-build basis. Completed in 2014, the headquarters required secure, resilient, and operationally reliable building systems for police administration, operations, and specialized support spaces.

MCW Consultants delivered mechanical and electrical engineering services for the Saskatoon Police Headquarters under the EllisDon design-build team. Scope included secure, mission-oriented systems, resilient power, operational reliability, and specialized support spaces appropriate to a police headquarters environment.

MCW's Role

MCW's mechanical and electrical scope addressed the elevated resilience, security, and reliability baseline required of a 24/7 police headquarters. Engineering decisions on power redundancy, life-safety systems, and HVAC accounted for continuous operation, secure-area requirements, and specialized support functions.

How Design-Build Was Applied

Public-safety design-build elevates the resilience and security baseline well above standard office or institutional projects. MCW's role under the DB consortium was to lock mechanical and electrical decisions early in coordination with the contractor and to ensure systems supported secure 24/7 operations.

Why this project matters

This project is a strong public-safety design-build reference for police, emergency response, and civic clients seeking engineering experience on secure, mission-oriented facilities.
