Overview

Rogers Place is Edmonton's major sports and entertainment arena and a central component of the city's downtown arena district. Delivered through design-build, the facility required highly coordinated mechanical systems to support large crowds, event flexibility, ice operations, concessions, hospitality, and back-of-house functions.

MCW Hemisphere served as Mechanical Engineer of Record on Rogers Place, delivering arena-grade mechanical systems including ice plant integration, large-volume ventilation, comfort heating and cooling, concessions and back-of-house systems, and event-flexibility provisions across the venue.

MCW's Role

As Mechanical Engineer of Record, MCW Hemisphere was accountable for the full mechanical scope from concept through commissioning. The arena's program demands — rapid event turnover, simultaneous ice and crowd operations, hospitality and concession layers, and back-of-house support — required disciplined coordination with the architect, structural engineer, and arena trades.

How Design-Build Was Applied

Sports-and-entertainment design-build is technically and operationally demanding — mechanical decisions affect crowd comfort, ice quality, event turnover, and operating cost simultaneously. MCW Hemisphere's role required disciplined coordination with the architect, structural engineer, and arena trades from concept through commissioning.

Why this project matters

This is a marquee design-build reference for large public-assembly, sports, entertainment, and civic-venue pursuits where sophisticated mechanical design and construction coordination are central to success.
