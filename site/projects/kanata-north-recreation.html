Overview

The Kanata North Recreation Complex is a municipal recreation facility delivered through design-build for the City of Ottawa. The complex required integrated M&E systems to support recreational programming, public access, community use, life safety, and operational efficiency.

MCW provided mechanical and electrical engineering for the recreation complex under the design-build delivery model, addressing the diverse program of pool, fitness, gymnasium, multi-purpose, and community spaces under one coordinated systems approach.

MCW's Role

MCW's mechanical and electrical scope covered the full recreation program: aquatics ventilation and water-treatment integration, pool and natatorium HVAC, gymnasium ventilation and lighting, fitness and multi-purpose room systems, and the public-access lobby and back-of-house areas. Engineering decisions were made with attention to operational efficiency, durability under high public use, and long-term maintainability for City operations staff.

How Design-Build Was Applied

Municipal recreation design-build requires the engineering team to coordinate program-driven systems (aquatics, fitness, gymnasium) with public-sector accountability for cost, schedule, and lifecycle performance. MCW's role under the DB framework was to lock systems decisions early in coordination with the contractor and to deliver a facility the City could operate and maintain efficiently from day one.

Why this project matters

This profile is suitable for municipal recreation and community-centre proposals where clients value design-build experience, public-sector accountability, and integrated M&E delivery.
