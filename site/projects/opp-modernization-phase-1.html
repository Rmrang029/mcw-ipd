Overview

OPP Modernization Phase 1 delivered new Ontario Provincial Police facilities across multiple Ontario communities through a DBFM public-private partnership. The portfolio included detachments, forensic identification units, regional command centres, and specialized OPP facilities, all targeting LEED Silver certification.

MCW served as the consortium engineer alongside NORR Architects under Bird Design-Build Construction, providing mechanical and electrical engineering across the Phase 1 facility portfolio.

MCW's Role

MCW's mechanical and electrical scope addressed the security-sensitive program of the OPP facilities — including detachments, forensic identification units, and command centres. Engineering decisions accommodated post-disaster operational requirements, secure operational areas, complex IT and communications systems, and the public-vs-secure-space separation typical of contemporary police facilities. Across the portfolio, MCW's role was anchored in repeatable engineering standards that could be applied consistently across geographically distributed sites.

How DBFM Was Applied

The DBFM model put the consortium responsible for design, construction, financing, and long-term maintenance of the facilities. MCW's mechanical and electrical scope had to perform reliably under contractual maintenance metrics across decades of operation, while still meeting the consortium's first-cost commitments. Multi-site portfolio delivery required disciplined standardization to keep engineering and constructed quality consistent across all locations.

Why this project matters

This is a useful reference for multi-site government, public-safety, and justice-sector programs where repeatability, lifecycle performance, and consistent engineering standards are essential. Phase 1 also paired naturally with Phase 2 (also delivered by the Bird-led team with MCW) as a demonstrated track record on repeat OPP portfolio work.
