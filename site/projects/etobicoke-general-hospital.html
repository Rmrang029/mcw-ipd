Overview

Etobicoke General Hospital's Phase 1 redevelopment delivered a new patient tower and major hospital expansion through a DBFM public-private partnership. The new wing opened in 2019 and added contemporary healthcare capacity for the William Osler Health System community.

MCW delivered mechanical, electrical, and energy modelling services on the Phase 1 patient tower and expansion under the DBFM consortium, addressing clinical-grade systems, healthcare code compliance, sustainability targets, and coordination with the operating hospital during phased construction.

MCW's Role

MCW's scope included mechanical, electrical, and energy modelling consulting for the Phase 1 patient tower. Healthcare environments require strict code compliance (CSA Z317 series, infection-control air-change rates, redundant power), and MCW's design balanced these against the consortium's lifecycle responsibilities and the hospital's operational continuity needs during construction.

How DBFM Was Applied

A 30-year DBFM hospital agreement requires that engineered systems perform reliably across decades of clinical use under contractual performance metrics. MCW's scope therefore had to balance capital cost, construction efficiency, and long-term clinical and operational performance.

Why this project matters

This project is a strong healthcare reference for P3/AFP pursuits where clinical functionality, facility maintenance, and sustainability credentials all factor into the owner's evaluation.
