Why MCW for IPD

Collaboration is a habit for us, not a learning curve.

IPD only works when every seat at the table can co-design under target cost, share risk fairly, and move at the speed of a Big Room. That requires a consultant fluent in lean delivery, target value design, BIM-driven coordination, and behavioural collaboration — not just a firm willing to attend more meetings.

Our Vancouver office leads pursuits in BC and is supported by MCW's national IPD experience, which includes some of Canada's earliest IPD-delivered schools and a portfolio that now spans civic, public-safety, post-secondary, and K-12 work.

What you can expect

Three things owners and architects tell us they value.

01

Decisions, not deliverables

Our IPD seniors arrive with the authority to commit. Big Room time is spent moving the design forward, not shuttling questions back to head office.

02

Constructability over cleverness

We design for the trades that will install it. Mechanical rooms, ceiling cavities, and service routing are coordinated for real-world build, with the contractor in the room as we draw.

03

Risk-reward fluency

We have operated under CCDC 30 and similar IPD agreements. We understand target cost, ICL pools, and how to keep a team's incentives aligned when design pressure climbs.
